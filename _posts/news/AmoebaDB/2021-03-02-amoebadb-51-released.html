New data in {{project}} 51

Proteomics

  • Oxidized proteomes after exposure to metronidazole and auranofin – Large-scale identification and quantification of the oxidised proteins formed in auranofin or metronidazole treated E. histolytica HM-1:IMSS trophozoites using resin-assisted capture coupled to mass spectrometry (MS).

New features in {{project}} 51

  • OrthoMCL DB now shares web architecture with other VEuPathDB sites. The updated and streamlined interface emphasizes easy access to tools and help information.
    • OrthoMCL now includes orthology information for 564 species, including all of the VEuPathDB organisms with annotated genomes.
    • The redesigned home page includes a new site-wide search for easily finding groups, proteins or documents. A categorized list of Searches appears on the left of the home page and the center section is devoted to tutorials for getting you started with OrthoMCL features.
    • Group and protein record pages are redesigned and include a contents section for navigating to the data you are interested in.
    • For more information see the OrthoMCL DB About page, the FAQ page or complete this tutorial.
  • Community annotation via Apollo is now available in {{project}}. Apollo is an open source software enabling users to inspect, refine and add gene models to the current genome annotations. Community annotation offers a value-added perspective to curation, especially for genomes without actively funded curation efforts. To contribute structural gene model changes, users inspect data as JBrowse tracks, create new or modified gene models, and save them in Apollo. To access Apollo, navigate to the Tools menu in the {{project}} header. To learn more, see this webinar recording describing manual gene annotation in Apollo.
