We are pleased to announce the release of ClinEpiDB 16!

New Features

  • We've made it easier to find a specific study! We realized the list of studies we have just keeps getting longer, so we added a search bar to the top of the "Search a Study" drop down menu so you don't have to scroll to find the study that you're interested in.
  • For studies with data access restrictions in place, we now publish the total number of data access requests they've received to date, the number of requests approved, and the percent approved. Find this information in the "Data Access Statistics" table on the study page or add it as columns on the "All Studies" page.

New Data

  • WASH Benefits provides rigorous evidence on the health and developmental benefits of water quality, sanitation, handwashing, and nutritional interventions during the first years of life. Primary outcomes data from the two cluster-randomized controlled trials, WASH-b Bangladesh and WASH-b Kenya, are now available!
  • The Program for Resistance, Immunology, Surveillance and Modeling of Malaria in Uganda 2 (PRISM2) study has new variables available related to malaria transmission and parasite haplotypes! Search for "membrane feeding" to see the results of mosquito membrane feeding assays or "complexity" to look at the parasite complexity of infection.
  • The Performance of Rotavirus and Oral Polio Vaccines in Developing Countries (PROVIDE) study has undergone some minor data updates. Data on a handful of stool samples has been added.
