We are pleased to announce the release of CryptoDB 41

New data in CryptoDB 41

Genome Sequence

  • Cryptosporidium sp. chipmunk LX-2015 —
    Cryptosporidium chipmunk genotype I is an emerging zoonotic pathogen. Guo et al.

Whole genome sequencing of isolates

  • Genomic variation in patient-derived Cryptosporidium hominis samples — Sikora et al.
  • Cryptosporidium hominis genetic diversity in isolates from Bangladesh —
    Genome sequencing of Cryptosporidium hominis from infants in slum dwelling from Dhaka Bangladesh. Gilchrist et al.

RNA sequence

  • Transcriptome of Cryptosporidium parvum intracellular stages —
    The transcriptome of cultured MDBK cells infected with Cryptosporidium parvum was analyzed by Illumina RNA sequencing. Four to five replicate samples of MDBK cells were sequenced 2h, 24h, and 48h post-infection. Data are provided prepublication from the Widmer Lab at Tufts University.
  • Transcriptome of Cryptosporidium hominis oocysts —
    Oocysts were analyzed by Illumina RNA sequencing. Data are provided prepublication from the Widmer Lab at Tufts University.
  • Transcriptome of Cryptosporidium parvum sporozoites treated with GalNAc —
    C. parvum sporozoites were incubated in either PBS or 10 µM GalNac for 0h (2 replicates each) or 2h (5 replicates each) and analyzed by Illumina RNA sequencing. Data are provided prepublication from the Widmer Lab at Tufts University.

New features in CryptoDB 41

Introducing Clustal Omega


EuPathDB sites are now using Clustal Omega for gene page protein alignments in the 'Orthologs and Paralogs within EuPathDB' table and the 'Retrieve multiple sequence alignment or multi-FASTA' tool in the 'Orthology and synteny' section of gene pages. The 'Isolate Alignments in this Gene Region' tool still uses CulstalW.

New Gene page tools

  • Interpro —
    The 'Protein features and properties' section now includes an option to submit the protein sequence for sequence analysis and classification via InterPro.
  • GPI anchor prediction —
    The 'Protein features and properties' section now includes an option to submit the protein sequence for GPI modification site prediction via the GPI Lipid Anchor Project.
