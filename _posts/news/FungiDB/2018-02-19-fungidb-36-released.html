We are pleased to announce the release of FungiDB 36

New data in this release

Genome Sequence and Annotation: these annotated genomes appear for the first time in FungiDB.

  • Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cubense tropical race 4 54006 – Provider: Li-Jun Ma; genome provided prior to publication. NCBI BioProject PRJNA73539.
  • Fusarium oxysporum Fo47 – Provider: Li-Jun Ma; genome provided prior to publication. NCBI BioProject PRJNA67069.
  • Histoplasma capsulatum G217B – Voorhies et al. 2015
  • Histoplasma capsulatum H88 (Ajellomyces capsulatus H88) – The Broad Institute. NCBI Bioproject PRJNA29163.
  • Penicillium rubens Wisconsin 54-1255 – van den Berg M. A. et al. 2011

Transcriptomics data

  • Transcriptome profiling of Coccidioides posadasii WT and Δcps1 mutant – Spherules were collected from both strains. Paired-end RNA-Seq analysis was performed in duplicates. Narra et al. 2008
  • Transcriptome analysis of the filamentous fungus Aspergillus nidulans directed to the global identification of promoters – A. nidulans transcriptome was accessed at 5 different growth conditions: nitrate, complete, ammonia, -N (4hr), and -N (72hr) media. Sibthorp et al. 2013
  • Microarray of Fusarium graminearum transcriptome during symptomless, symptomatic, and intermediate wheat disease symptoms – Three replicates were collected from rachis internodes and compared to spikelet samples. Brown et al. 2017

Phenotype data

  • Phenotypic analysis of Neurospora crassa knockout mutants – A phenotypic pipeline was designed for high-throughput analysis of 10 different traits during hyphal growth, asexual sporulation (macroconidiation), and the sexual cycle in knockout mutants from Neurospora crassa. Provider: Katherine Borkovich; dataset provided prior to publication. Borkovich et al.
  • Neurospora Genome Project Phenotype Image Collection – Analysis of colony growth and morphology was performed on solid Vogel's minimal (MIN) medium. For each strain, the hyphae at the edge of the colony and plate growth were photographed. Dunlap et al. 2007, Colot et al. 2006, and Turner G.E. 2011.

Proteomics data

  • Proteogenomic Re-Annotation of Coccidioides posadasii str. Silveira – Protein fractions of in vitro grown mycelia and parasitic phase spherules from C. posadasii str. Silveira were analysed using GeLC-MS/MS. Proteomic analysis was performed to provide empirical evidence of the proteins produced in both the mycelial (CDN) and parasitic (SPH) phases. Mitchell et al.

Updated Phenotype data for Aspergillus species:

  • Manually curated Aspergillus Phenotypes – The literature-curated phenotype data from AspGD have now been completely loaded onto FungiDB. The data have been reformatted in-line with EuPathDB's approach for phenotype annotation, where we follow an "entity + quality" approach. We have converted the phenotypes from AspGD-curated Ascomycete Phenotype Ontology (APO) to multiple ontologies: Phenotypic Quality Ontology (PATO) for "quality" terms and Gene Ontology (GO), Fungal Gross Anatomy Ontology (FAO), Chemical Entities of Biological Interest (ChEBI) and Ascomycete Phenotype Ontology (APO) for "entity" terms. We also plan to improve the phenotype table (in gene pages) and the query structure in the coming releases. See table below for details.

Functional Annotations from Other Databases - FgMutantDB

A total 1,247 user comments matching 1,158 unique GeneIDs were loaded from FgMutantDb. Comments contain information pertaining F. graminearum mutants, gene products, publication references, etc. User comments can be searched by deploying a New Search for Genes, Annotations, Curation, and Identifiers, User comments and then limiting the search results on F. graminearum.

New features in this release

Search Page Improvements: search pages were redesigned to include an organism filter and provide better access to help information.

  • Search parameters are now left justified. The Get Answer button remains center justified below the parameters. Parameter help icons, which offer information concerning the parameters when hovered over, now appear before the parameter name instead of after.
  • The Organism Parameter, the first parameter on search pages, has a new look and now contains a filter box for quickly finding your organism of interest. Type any portion of an organism name (at leaset two letters of the genus, species, or strain) in the filter box and the tree collapses to show only those organisms containing your entry. To return to the full tree, click the X in the filter box.

New Paralog Count search

  • The new Paralog Count search returns genes based on the number of assigned paralogs. Find genes that have a number of paralogs within the specified range. Paralogs are defined as genes from the same species that belong to the same OrthoMCL group.
  • Find the search under the Orthology and Synteny category.

Gene Record Page Improvements:

  • A new GO Slim table now appears above the GO Term table on the record pages of genes with GO associated data. The GO Slim table maps GO Terms form the GO Term table to their GO Slim terms based on the Generic GO slim developed by GO Consortium.
  • Column headers in gene page tables now show a help icon ( ) when additional information or definitions are available for the column. Hover over the help icons to reveal additional information.
  • Metabolic pathway and reaction tables on gene pages now include reaction compound names, with CHEBI IDs on mouse over, and a column that indicates if the EC number associated with a gene is an exact match to the EC number in metabolic pathway.

Search Strategy Improvement: new option for combining steps in a search strategy.

  • Search strategies now offer an option to 'ignore' a step when editing a multi-step strategy. To remove a search from the strategy without deleting the step, click Edit for the combine graphic of the step you are interested in ignoring. Then choose 'IGNORE step #' from the Revise Operation popup. For more information see this Ignore Step help document.

Introducing ClinEpiDB, a new member of the EuPathDB family of databases:

  • We are excited to announce the launch of ClinEpiDB, which hosts clinical and epidemiological data for the infectious disease research community. The current release contains data from the PRISM study on malaria in Uganda. Check out this video to learn more about our newest EuPathDB resource!


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
