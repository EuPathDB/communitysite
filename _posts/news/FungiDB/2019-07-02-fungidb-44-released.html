New data in FungiDB 44

Genome Sequence and Annotation: these annotated genomes appear for the first time in FungiDB.

  • Cladophialophora immunda CBS 83496 – Teixeira et al. 2017.
  • Rhizophagus irregularis DAOM 181602 (DAOM 197198) – Chen et al. 2018, Ropars et al. 2016, Tisserant et al. 2013.

New Genome Sequence: available for the first time in FungiDB:

  • Amauroascus niger UAMH 3544 – Whiston et al. 2015.

Transcriptomics

  • Aspergillus fumigatus transcriptome under normoxia and hypoxia conditions – RNAseq analysis of the Aspergillus fumigatus was used to determine its response to early hypoxic conditions. Losada et al. 2014.
  • Transcriptome landscape of Candida glabrata during pH change and nitrosative stress – RNA-Seq-based analysis of the transcriptomic landscape of C. glabrata in nutrient-rich media, under nitrosative stress, and during pH shift. Linde et al. 2015.
  • Microarray analysis of fruiting body development in Coprinopsis cinerea – Meiosis was compared in rad50-1 and wild type strains of C. cinerea at 6 time points spanning the meiotic timecourse. Anderson et al. 2012.
  • O2-responsive genes in the human pathogenic fungus Aspergillus fumigatus – Transcriptomic investigation of low oxygen adaptation of A. fumigatus. Hillmann et al. 2014.
  • Histoplasma capsulatum G186A and G217A yeast and mycelial transcriptomes – Yeast and mycelial transcriptome of Histoplasma capsulatum G186A and G217A strains. Edwards et al. 2013.
  • Gene expression analysis of ADV-1 and PP-1 mutants in Neurospora crassa – The transcription factors ADV-1 and PP-1 are essential for germling communication and fusion and Neurospora crassa. Fischer et al. 2018.

Phenotype data

  • Manually curated Cryptococcus Phenotypes – The phenotypes for Cryptococcus neoformans JEC21 and H99 and Cryptococcus gatii WM276 are manually curated from published literature describing phenotypes obtained from mutant studies. We have curated and added phenotypes from Lee et al. publication on kinases to the currently existing phenotypic dataset for C. neoformans H99. We followed an "entity + quality" approach and curated phenotypes with multiple ontologies: Phenotypic Quality Ontology (PATO) for "quality" terms and Gene Ontology (GO), Fungal Gross Anatomy Ontology (FAO), Chemical Entities of Biological Interest (ChEBI) and Ascomycete Phenotype Ontology (APO) for "entity" terms.

New features in FungiDB 44

  • Synteny and the Protein Browser are availabe in our beta implementation of the JBrowse Genome Browser.
  • New EuPathDB RNA Seq Tool. The EuPathDB Galaxy Workspace now offers a tool that converts read counts from HTSeq-count output to FPKM values.
  • New EuPathDB Workflows. The EuPathDB Galaxy Workspace is updated with several new pre-configured workflows that are designed to allow export of strand specific data to EuPathDB for visualization and searching.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
