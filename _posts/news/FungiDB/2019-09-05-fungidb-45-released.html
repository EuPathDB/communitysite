New data in FungiDB 45

Genome Sequence and Annotation: these annotated genomes appear for the first time in FungiDB.

  • Trichoderma virens Gv29-8 – Kubicek et al. 2011.
  • Exophiala mesophila CBS 40295 – Tafer et al. 2015.
  • Candida duobushaemulonis B09383 – Chow et al. 2018.
  • Cenococcum geophilum 1.58 – Peter et al. 2016.

New Genome Sequence: available for the first time in FungiDB:

  • Chrysosporium queenslandicum CBS 280.77 – Whiston et al. 2015.
  • Byssoonygena ceratinophila UAMH 5669 – Whiston et al. 2015.

Transcriptomics

  • Transcriptome analysis of the col-26 mutant and its wild type parental strain – Neurospora crassa: mRNA profiles after 4 hours of exposure to various carbon sources. Xiong et al. 2017.
  • WT and DAMT3 mutant transcriptomes – Phytophthora sojae: RNA-Seq analysis of the mycelium stage. Zhao et al. 2014.
  • Genome-wide gene expression analysis of spores and mycelium – Fusarium graminearum: Examination of gene expression in two developmental stages. Anderson et al. 2012.
  • Global transcriptional analysis of wild-type and cuf1delta cells – Cryptococcus neoformans: Genome-wide assessment of the Cu-responsive, Cuf1-dependent transcriptome. Garcia-Santamarina et al. 2018.
  • Transcriptional analysis of sorted subpopulations of macrophages infected with Candida albicans – RNA-Seq of C57BL6J macrophages infected with the CAI4 strain of Candida albicans. Muñoz et al. 2019.

Whole genome sequence of isolates for SNP analysis:

  • Aligned genome sequence reads - C. neoformans Zambian environmental and clinical isolates – Forty five isolates were sequenced using Illumina HiSeq 2000 technology. Vanhove et al. 2017.

Proteomics

  • Adaptive mechanisms of conidia to nutrient restriction in – A clinical strain was grown for 3, 15, and 30 days to follow the changes associated with conidia adaptation to nutrient limitation. Anjo et al. 2017.

DAP-Seq data

  • DAP-Seq performed on ADV-1 and PP-1 proteins in Neurospora crassa – To identify genes that were regulated and bound by ADV-1 or PP-1, DNA-affinity purification sequencing (DAP-seq) was used to identify transcription factor binding sites in N. crassa. Fischer et al. 2018.

Phenotype data

  • Manually curated Cryptococcus Phenotypes – The phenotypes for Cryptococcus neoformans JEC21 and H99 and Cryptococcus gatii WM276 are manually curated from published literature describing phenotypes obtained from mutant studies. We have curated and added phenotypes from Bairwa et al. 2019. publication on kinases to the currently existing phenotypic dataset for C. neoformans H99. We followed an "entity + quality" approach and curated phenotypes with multiple ontologies: Phenotypic Quality Ontology (PATO) for "quality" terms and Gene Ontology (GO), Fungal Gross Anatomy Ontology (FAO), Chemical Entities of Biological Interest (ChEBI) and Ascomycete Phenotype Ontology (APO) for "entity" terms.

New features in FungiDB 45

  • All EuPathDB sites now offer the JBrowse Genome Browser. GBrowse will be phased out in coming releases. Please Contact Us with comments and suggestions for the genome browser.
  • The new RNASeq Transcript Summary tool on gene pages helps reveal experiments in which the gene is highly regulated. The tool displays the gene's FPKM values for all experiments in FungiDB and offers features such as log/linear scaling, zoom and pan to explore the gene's expression in each experiment.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
