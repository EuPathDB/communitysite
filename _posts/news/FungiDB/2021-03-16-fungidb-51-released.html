New data in FungiDB 51

EBI Pipeline

{{project}} 51 represents the first release that employs the EBI pipeline for analyzing genomes and RNA-Seq data. ({{project}} Analysis Methods) All genomes were run through the EBI pipeline, updating all analyses such as InterproScan and repeat identification. While much of the EBI pipeline is the same as our previous in-house pipeline, these differences are noted:
  • Expression values calculated from RNA-Seq data are now presented as TPM (Transcripts Per Kilobase Million) instead of FPKM (Fragments Per Kilobase Million) or RPKM (Reads Per Kilobase Million). Learn more
  • Signal peptide predictions are now made with SignalP-4.1, updated from SignalP-3.0.
  • All pseudogenes now include automatically predicted UTRs.

  • Fresh whole blood infected with Candida tropicalis — RNA-pool of both species extracted at 0min (control), 15, 30, 60, 120 and 240 min. Sieber et al. 2018. The host component of this data set is available on HostDB: Fresh human whole blood infected with Candida tropicalis.
  • Total RNA profiles of cells grown in glucose or GlcNAc medium — Transcriptomic changes occurring during yeast-filament transition in the pathogen fungus Candida tropicalis MYA-3404. Zhang et al. 2016.
  • RNA-Seq analyses of fruiting body development — Developmental transcriptome of a multicellular basidiomycete Coprinopsis cinerea. Krizsán et al. 2019.
  • Transcriptional landscape of Cryptococcus - host response — Animal infection model using the mouse Mus musculus to study the host response to infection. Li et al. 2019.
  • Gene expression variation in response to biologically relevant stresses — Global transcriptomic changes in the pathogen Crypococcus neoformans occurring in response to host temperature stress. Yu et al. 2020.
  • Mouse macrophages infected with Mucor circinelloides — A study of the transcriptomics changes occuring in both host and pathogen upon the interaction of Mucor circinelloides spores with phagocytic cells of a mouse. Pérez-Arques et al. 2019.

Phenotype data

  • Manually Curated Aspergillus Phenotypes – The currently existing phenotypes for Aspergillus species were manually curated by AspGD from published literature describing phenotypes obtained from mutant studies. These phenotypic annotations were reformatted at FungiDB in-line with VEuPathDB’s approach for phenotype curation. We have curated and integrated phenotypes from Furukawa et al. 2020 for Aspergillus fumigatus Af293 and from Frawley et al. 2018 for Aspergillus nidulans FGSC A4.

  • Manually Curated Pyricularia Phenotypes – The phenotypes for Pyricularia oryzae 70-15 are manually curated from published literature describing phenotypes obtained from mutant studies. These phenotypes have been manually curated in-line with VEuPathDB's approach for phenotype annotation and integrated into FungiDB. We followed an "entity + quality" approach and curated phenotypes with multiple ontologies: Phenotypic Quality Ontology (PATO) for "quality" terms and Gene Ontology (GO), Fungal Gross Anatomy Ontology (FAO), Chemical Entities of Biological Interest (ChEBI) and Ascomycete Phenotype Ontology (APO) for "entity" terms. We have curated and integrated phenotypes from Guo et al. 2010, Guo et al. 2011, Lu et al. 2014 and Kong et al. 2015 publications for bZIP and Zn2Cys6 transcription factors.

New features in {{project}} 51

  • BLASTbeta tool – a new BLAST tool with multi-BLAST capabilities. Input a single sequence or several FASTA-formatted sequences at once. Our tool uses NCBI BLAST to find genes, genomic sequences, ESTs, or PopSet Sequences that have BLAST similarity to your input sequence. BLASTbeta video introduction (4 min)
  • Usage and performance metrics for VEuPathDB sites are now updated monthly.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
