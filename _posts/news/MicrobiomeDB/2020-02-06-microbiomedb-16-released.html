We are pleased to announce the release of MicrobiomeDB 16

New data in this release

  • A study of a birth cohort of 271 children from the Amazonian lowlands near Iquitos, Peru, for which stool samples were collected at postnatal months 6, 12, 18, and 24". This cohort has a high prevalence of growth stunting and diarrhea, and is one of the participating sites in the MAL-ED study.

New features in this release

  • The search box on the landing page now searches both the text on each study card, as well as the the more extensive 'study details' page. This means that users can now easily filter cards based on principal investigator, publications, institution, and more.
  • Users now have the ability to customize histogram displays for any sample detail (metadata) variable in the filtering tools, including rescaling X and Y axis, toggling between log and linear displays, and customized binning.
  • The search strategy has been redesigned for a better user experience.
Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits.
