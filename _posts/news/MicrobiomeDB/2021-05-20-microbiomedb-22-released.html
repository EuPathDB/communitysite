We are pleased to announce the release of MicrobiomeDB 22

New data in this release

  • The neonatal intensive care, necrotizing enterocolitis (NICU NEC) study includes shotgun metagenomic data from over 1100 stool samples collected from 150 infants born prematurely.
  • All available shotgun metagenomic data from phase I of the Human Microbiome Project (HMP), including over 700 samples collected from over 100 healthy adult volunteers at multiple body sites.

New features in this release

  • Our relative abundance app now allows users to choose a ranking method for displaying top n taxa, including median, maximum, third quartile, and variance.
  • Our differential abundance app now includes a 'run analysis' button that allows users to select desired parameters before actually runnning DESeq2 to identify differentially abundant taxa.
  • Our correlation app has been redesigned to incorporate multiple UI/UX improvements.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits.
