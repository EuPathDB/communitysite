--- layout: post title: MicrosporidiaDB 4.0 released category: [EuPathDB,VEuPathDB,MicrosporidiaDB] --- We are pleased to announce the release of MicrosporidiaDB 4.0

New data in this release:


RNA-sequencing of N. parisii ERT1 infection in C. elegans (Cuomo et al.)
  • Search this dataset to find N. parisii genes that are differentially expressed between time points during the first 64 hours post-infection or by their percentile expression. 
  • View RNA-Seq reads aligned to the genome with the GBrowse track:  N. parisii Infection Time Series (log2) (Cuomo et al.). 
  • View a plot of transcript level vs. hours post-infection for individual N. parisii genes in the Expression section of gene pages. 
  • Combine this dataset with other data in MicrosporidiaDB using a search strategy.  For example, this strategy identifies N. parisii genes that are upregulated late in the time course compared to early time points.

Genomic sequence and annotation for the following organisms:
  • Encephalitozoon romaleae SJ-2008 (Pombert et al.)
  • Vavraia culicis floridensis (The Broad Institute)
  • Vittaforma corneae ATCC 50505 (The Broad Institute)

Unannotated genomic sequence for:
  • Anncaliia algerae Undeen (Peyretaillade et al.)

New Functionality:

PCR Primer Design tool added to GBrowse.

Design PCR primers to any region of the genome using this interactive, graphic interface. To reach this tool, open the MicrosporidiaDB Genome Browser at your genomic region of interest, choose "Design PCR primers" from the dropdown menu and click "GO".  To design primers, focus on a PCR product size range by highlighting the area on the scale, then enter your preferred PCR parameters in the text boxes and click Design Primer.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary.  Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits.
