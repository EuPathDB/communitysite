New data in {{project}} 51

EBI Pipeline

{{project}} 51 represents the first release that employs the EBI pipeline for analyzing genomes and RNA-Seq data. ({{project}} Analysis Methods) All genomes were run through the EBI pipeline, updating all analyses such as InterproScan and repeat identification. While much of the EBI pipeline is the same as our previous in-house pipeline, these differences are noted:
  • Expression values calculated from RNA-Seq data are now presented as TPM (Transcripts Per Kilobase Million) instead of FPKM (Fragments Per Kilobase Million) or RPKM (Reads Per Kilobase Million). Learn more
  • Signal peptide predictions are now made with SignalP-4.1, updated from SignalP-3.0.

Whole genome sequencing of isolates

  • Six Babesia microti isolates – Whole genome sequencing reads aligned to the Babesia microti R1 genome and used to determine single nucleotide polymorphisms. From the lab of Joana Carneiro da Silva, University of Maryland School of Medicine.

New features in {{project}} 51

  • OrthoMCL DB now shares web architecture with other VEuPathDB sites. The updated and streamlined interface emphasizes easy access to tools and help information.
    • OrthoMCL now includes orthology information for 564 species, including all of the VEuPathDB organisms with annotated genomes.
    • The redesigned home page includes a new site-wide search for easily finding groups, proteins or documents. A categorized list of Searches appears on the left of the home page and the center section is devoted to tutorials for getting you started with OrthoMCL features.
    • Group and protein record pages are redesigned and include a contents section for navigating to the data you are interested in.
    • For more information see the OrthoMCL DB About page, the FAQ page or complete this tutorial.
