We are pleased to announce the release of PiroplasmaDB 2.0

This release of PiroplasmaDB constitutes a complete rebuild of the database.  This means that all genomes and data were reloaded and reanalyzed.  It is possible that many of your stored search strategies will need to be rerun to accommodate the new analyses (please see note at the bottom of this news item).  We encourage you to explore this release and, as usual, we look forward to hearing from you with suggestions for further improvement and any problems you may encounter.

New data in this release:

  • Babesia bovis microarray data from Lau et. al. Incorporation of this data enables you to search for genes that are differentially regulated between virulent and attenuated strains of B. bovis.  Searches against this data are available by selection "Microarray" in the "Transcript Expression" section under the heading "Identify Genes by" on the home page.

Searches for gens based on their expression profile may be combined with other available searches in PiroplasmaDB.  For example, the following shared search strategy identifies predicted secreted genes that are differentially regulated between virulent and attenuated strains:

http://piroplasmadb.org/piro/im.do?s=eb3dc45918c75c68

In addition, graphical representations of expression profiles are available on all B. bovis gene pages.  As an example, you can visit the gene page for one of the variant erythrocyte surface antigens and scroll down to the expression section.

  • Isolate data from environmental samples, Theileriidae and Babesiidae obtained from Genbank are now available.  These data may be searched based on multiple criteria including geographic location, isolation source, host, strain, etc.  Searches against isolate data are available under the "Isolates" section under the heading "Identify other data types" on the home page.  Isolate searches may be combined such as the following strategy that identifies all Theileria isolates from Africa:
http://piroplasmadb.org/piro/im.do?s=2c545c1d461d5df3



New functionality:

Graphical representation of your gene results mapped to genomic sequences.  To use this feature, run any gene search then click on the "Genome View" tab.  This view allows you to interactively visualize the genomic distribution of your results -- you can zoom in/out, sort results or visit specific gene pages. 


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary.  Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ã˜) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
