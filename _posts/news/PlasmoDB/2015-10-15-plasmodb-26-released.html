We are pleased to announce the release of PlasmoDB 26

New data in this release

The data set title below links to a full description and list of associated searches and GBrowse tracks.

Genome Sequence and Annotation: the following genomes were updated to the current GeneDB versions

  • Plasmodium berghei ANKA — updated and improved assembly and annotation GeneDB version 6 July 2015. From the Parasite Genomics Group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. Data Use Policy.
  • Plasmodium chabaudi chabaudi — updated and improved assembly and annotation GeneDB version 18 June 2015. From the Parasite Genomics Group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. Data Use Policy.
  • Plasmodium knowlesi strain H — the genome was updated to the reassembled and reannotated GeneDB version 18 June 2015. From the Parasite Genomics Group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. Data Use Policy.

Functional Annotation Updated

Annotation updated: products, GO terms, EC numbers, gene names and synonyms
  • Plasmodium berghei ANKA — updated GeneDB version August 2015. 101 genes with updated annotations.
  • Plasmodium chabaudi chabaudi AS — updated GeneDB version August 2015. 115 genes with updated annotations.
  • Plasmodium falciparum 3D7 — updated GeneDB version August 2015. 506 genes with updated annotations.
  • Plasmodium knowlesi H — updated GeneDB version August 2015. 129 genes with updated annotations.
  • Plasmodium rechenowi CDC — updated GeneDB version August 2015. 302 genes with updated annotations.
  • Plasmodium yoelii yoelii 17X — updated GeneDB version August 2015. 312 genes with updated annotations.

Protein Array:

  • Treatment-time to reinfection cohort from Kisumu area, Kenya collected in 2003 — Protein microarrays were probed with plasma from residents of a community in Kenya monitored for 12 weeks for reinfection and symptomatic malaria after administration of antimalarial drugs. Dent et al..

Transcription Start Sites:

  • TSS from 5'cap-selected mRNA — Transcription Start Site locations for P.falciparum 3D7. Genome-wide transcription start sites mapping based on high-throughput sequencing of 5' cap-selected mRNA molecules isolated from 6 time points during P. falciparum 3D7 intra-erythrocytic asexual cycle. Lars Steinmetz (EMBL Heidelberg)
