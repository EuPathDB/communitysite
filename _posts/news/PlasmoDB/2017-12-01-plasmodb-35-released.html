We are pleased to announce the release of PlasmoDB 35

New data in this release

Genome Sequence and Annotation

  • Plasmodium knowlesi strain Malayan Strain Pk1 A – PacBio assembly of a Plasmodium knowlesi genome sequence with Hi-C correction and some manual annotation. First release of this genome in PlasmoDB. Lapp et al.

RNA Sequence

  • Female and male gametocyte transcriptomes – RNAseq data comparing transcript abundance for FACS sorted male and female P. berghei gametocytes compared these to a mixed asexual population. Yeoh et al.

Proteomics

  • Salivary gland sporozoite total and surface proteome (VK210, VK247) – Proteins identified from P. vivax salivary gland sporozoites, including putatively surface-exposed proteins identified by surface labeling with a membrane-impermeable biotin tag. Swearingen et al.

Protein Array

  • India ICEMR Antibody Array – Serological profiling of immune responses at three sites in India by probing protein microarrays consisting of 515 Plasmodium vivax and 500 Plasmodium falciparum proteins with 353 plasma samples. A total of 236 malaria-positive (symptomatic and asymptomatic) plasma samples and 117 malaria-negative samples were collected at three field sites in Raurkela, Nadiad, and Chennai. Indian samples showed significant seroreactivity to 265 P. vivax and 373 P. falciparum antigens, but overall seroreactivity to P. vivax antigens was lower compared to P. falciparum antigens. Uplekar et al.

New features in this release

Dedicated search for Genes with protein products that have post-translational modifications

PlasmoDB offers a new search 'Identify Genes based on Post-Translational Modifications, which returns genes whose protein products were experimentally determined to be post-translationally modified. This search is categorized under Search for Genes, Proteomics, Post-Translational Modification.

GO Slim Implementation

GO Slim terms are now indicated in EuPathDB sites in the following ways:
  • The search, Identify Genes based on GO Terms, now includes an GO Subset option for limiting the search to only GO Slim generic terms.
  • GO Terms tables on gene pages now contain a 'GO Subset' column which identifies GO Slim generic terms.
  • The GO enrichment analysis has an option for limiting the analysis to GO Slim terms. This will limit both the background and the gene list of interest in the Fisher's exact test that determines enrichment.

Revigo and Word Cloud access from GO Enrichment results

PlasmoDB offers a link between our GO Enrichment results and a Word Cloud visualization as well as Revigo, a tool that provides GO term summarization and visualization. The links to Revigo and the Word Cloud now appear as buttons that are right justified above the GO enrichment result table.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
