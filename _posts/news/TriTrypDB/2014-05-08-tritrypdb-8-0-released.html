We are pleased to announce the release of TriTrypDB 8.0

New data in this release:
  • Trypanosoma brucei TREU927 procyclic form origins of replication: Origins of replication were determined by Marker Frequency Analysis, a technique that compares copy number of the marker sequences in replicating and non-replicating cells. Origin-proximal sequences will be over-represented compared with origin-distal markers during replication (Tiengwe et al.). This data can be viewed in the genome browser. View experimental details in the dataset section.  

  • Trypanosoma brucei TREU927 transcriptome during RNAi knockdown of Orc1/Cdc6: RNAi knockdown of Orc1/Cdc6 was performed and RNA-seq used to measure transcript abundance (Tiengwe et al.). View experimental details and available searches in the dataset section. 

  • Draft genome sequence and annotation from the Trypanosomatid sequencing white paper project: This data is graciously provided prepublication and full credit and data usage information is available by clicking on the organism name:

    • Crithidia fasciculata Cf-C1

    • L. braziliensis MHOM/BR/75/M2903

  • Trypanosoma grayi ANR4 genome sequence and annotation: This data is graciously provided prepublication by Dr. Mark Field (Kelly et al.). View experimental details and available searches in the dataset section. 

  • Leishmania amazonensis MHOM/BR/71973/M2269 genome sequence (Real et al.): View experimental details and available searches in the dataset section. 

  • Trypanosoma cruzi CL Brener Esmeraldo-like comparative genomic hybridizations of 33 strains (Minning et al.): Genomic regions of copy number variation can be viewed in the genome browser. View experimental details in the dataset section. 

  • Trypanosoma brucei TREU927 procyclic form methylarginine proteome (Lott et al.):  The methylarginine proteome of mitochondrially enriched procyclic form T. brucei 29-13 cells was determined by LC-MS/MS.  View experimental details and available searches in the dataset section. 

  • Trypanosoma brucei TREU927 fractionated mitochondrial membrane and ER proteomes (Niemann et al.): Protein composition of Trypanosoma brucei mitochondrial membrane and endoplasmic reticulum fractions, including purified whole mitochondrial membranes (pure mitos), crude and purified outer membrane, crude inner membrane, crude endoplasmic reticulum, and mixed outer membrane/endiplasmic reticulum fractions.  View experimental details and links to searches in the dataset section. 

  • Trypanosoma brucei TREU927 mitochondrial methylarginine proteome (Fisk et al.): The mitochondrial methylarginine proteome of procyclic form T. brucei EATRO 164 Istar1 cells was determined by LC-MS/MS.  View experimental details and links to searches in the dataset section. 

  • Leishmania infantum JPCM5 Promastigote and Amastigote Phosphoproteomes (Tsigankov et al.): Phosphoproteomic analysis of L. donovani 1SR amastigotes and promastigotes. Data was researched against the L. infantum genome.  View experimental details and links to searches in the dataset section. 

  • Trypanosoma cruzi epimastigote cell surface proteome (Queiroz et al.): Differentially expressed proteins involved in T. cruzi Dm28c metacyclogenesis determined by quantitative proteomics.  View experimental details and links to searches in the dataset section.

  • Trypanosoma cruzi quantitative proteomics during metacyclogenesis (de Godoy et al.): Cell surface proteins were isolated by two methods and peptides analyzed by LC-MS/MS.  View experimental details and links to searches in the dataset section.

  • Trypanosoma cruzi SUMOylation enriched quantitative epimastigote proteomics (Bayona et al.): Quantitative proteomics was performed on SUMOylation enriched protein samples.  View experimental details and links to searches in the dataset section.


New features:
  • Search genes based on polymorphism data: The high throughput sequencing SNP searches have been upgraded to increase their speed and provide additional search functionality. Meta data associated with samples is now  more intuitively organized. Search for genes based on SNP characteristics or identify SNPs based on multiple criteria including within one group of isolates or between two groups of isolates.
  • Filter samples based on metadata: It is easier now to tailor your searches (such as SNP searches) by defining samples based on their metadata characteristics.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary.  Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
