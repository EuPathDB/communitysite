We are pleased to announce the release of TriTrypDB 35

New data in TriTrypDB 35

RNA Sequence

  • Circadian control of bloodstream and procyclic form transcriptome (AnTat1.1) — Bloodstream and procyclic form transcriptomes during a 48-hour time-course of alternating temperatures or at constant temperature. Rijo-Ferreira et al.

Proteomics

  • BSF and PCF Surface Proteomics (Tb427) — Trypanosoma brucei proteomics of the cell surface from both PCF and BSF stages Shimogawa et al.

Quantitative Proteomics

  • Nuclear Proteome (Tb427) — Nuclear proteome of T. brucei Goos et al.

New features in this release

Dedicated search for Genes with protein products that have post-translational modifications

TriTrypDB offers a new search, Identify Genes based on Post-Translational Modifications, which returns genes whose protein products were experimentally determined to be post-translationally modified. This search is categorized under Search for Genes, Proteomics, Post-Translational Modification.

GO Slim Implementation

GO Slim terms are now indicated in EuPathDB sites in the following ways:
  • The search, Identify Genes based on GO Terms, now includes an GO Subset option for limiting the search to only GO Slim generic terms.
  • GO Terms tables on gene pages now contain a 'GO Subset' column which identifies GO Slim generic terms.
  • The GO enrichment analysis has an option for limiting the analysis to GO Slim terms. This will limit both the background and the gene list of interest in the Fisher's exact test that determines enrichment.

Revigo and Word Cloud access from GO Enrichment results

TriTrypDB offers a link between our GO Enrichment results and a Word Cloud visualization as well as Revigo, a tool that provides GO term summarization and visualization. The links to Revigo and the Word Cloud now appear as buttons that are right justified above the GO enrichment result table.

News on annotation

Personnel and gene page comments

We are pleased to inform that David Starns recently joined the EuPathDB team as an annotator/curator to replace Fatima Silva on her departure from the project. Achchuthan Shanmugasundram (achchu@liverpool.ac.uk) and David Starns (D.E.Starns@liverpool.ac.uk) are curating TriTrypDB genomes and can be contacted regarding functional annotation.

Gene page comments that are entered by users are actively curated at GeneDB. Since the Kinetoplastid Molecular Cell Biology Meeting 2017, we devoted substantial effort to curating gene page comments submitted for Trypanosoma brucei 927 genome and updating functional annotations (see table below). These annotations are currently available in GeneDB and will be integrated as functional annotations in TriTrypDB 36.
