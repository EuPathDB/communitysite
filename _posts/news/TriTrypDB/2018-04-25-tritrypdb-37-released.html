We are pleased to announce the release of TriTrypDB 37

New data in TriTrypDB 37

Genome sequence and annotation

  • Paratrypanosoma confusum CUL13 — This genome sequence and annotation appears for the first time in TriTrypDB. Skalicky et al.
  • Leishmania panamensis MHOM/PA/94/PSC-1 — This genome sequence and annotation appears for the first time in TriTrypDB. Llanes et al.

RNA Sequencing

  • Leishmania major RNAseq during human macrophage infection — RNAseq analysis of transcriptomes of human macrophage and parasite during an infection time course. Human macrophage were infected with Leishmania major and Leishmania amazonensis. Fernandes et al. This data set has corresponding host data at HostDB: Leishmania major and Leishmania amazonensis RNAseq during human macrophage infection

Chromatin Imunoprecipitation Sequence

  • Nucleosome positions in Tb427 bloodstream and procyclic forms — Genome-wide nucleosome positions in T. brucei Lister 427 bloodstream form and procyclic form using MNase-seq. Maree et al.

Cellular Localization Imaging

  • Cellular Localization of tagged T. brucei proteins — Additional images and localization information associated with GO terms were obtained from the TryTag Project, bringing the total number of genes with TrypTag images to 4,404 genes. Access the data on gene pages (example) and through a dedicated search based on GO terms: Identify Genes based on Cellular Localization Imaging. Dean et al.

Curated User Comments

Gene page comments that are entered by users are curated at GeneDB and regularly integrated as functional annotations in TriTrypDB. Since the Kinetoplastid Molecular Cell Biology Meeting 2017, we devoted substantial effort to curating gene page comments submitted for Trypanosoma brucei 927 and Leishmania species and to updating functional annotations. 236 user comments (unique) have been integrated in this release.

New features in this release

Beta EuPathDB Workspace

  • Announcing the Beta release of EuPathDB Workspace, a private workspace that can interface with EuPathDB Galaxy and allows for viewing and interrogating private data against data that is already integrated into EuPathDB (public data). The first EuPathDB workspace tool, 'Bigwig files to EuPathDB' exports bigwig files from EuPathDB Galaxy to EuPathDB. The tool creates a EuPathDB Data Set which is managed from the My Data Sets page along with your other private data sets. Each data set also receives a record page which lists all files that the data set contains, offers file management functions and ports files to the genome browser for viewing.

    Video tutorial: Export Bigwig files to EuPathDB

Pathway Improvements

  • Semantic zooming of Pathway drawings on Pathway record pages - At low zoom levels, pathway nodes appear as glyphs. At mid zoom levels, the main compound nodes appear as molecular formulas. At high zoom levels, enzymes and side nodes appear as formulas, terms or Enzyme commission numbers. Clicking on a node will retrieve details about the node, regardless of zoom level.


Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
