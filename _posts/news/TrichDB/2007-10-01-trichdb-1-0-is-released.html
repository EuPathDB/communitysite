--- layout: post title: TrichDB 1.0 is released category: [TrichDB] --- We are pleased to announce the release of TrichDB (http://trichdb.org ) a new Web resource for interrogating the Trichomonas vaginalis genome, which was sequenced and annotated at The Institute for Genomics Research and published in Science magazine in early 2007.

TrichDB is modeled after the ApiDB family of apicomplexan genome database query sites (http://www.apidb.org). Like those sites, TrichDB offers researchers a plethora of tools for genomic data mining. In addition to BLAST and pattern/motif similarity searches, users can identify T. vaginalis genes by genomic position; common name or keyword; gene attributes such as gene type and exon number; transcript expression evidence (ESTs including prepublication ESTs kindly provided by Dr. Petrus Tang and collaborators); physical characteristics of predicted proteins such as molecular weight and secondary structure; predicted function from Gene Ontology and EC enzyme number annotation; and cellular location. Along with these tools for gene analysis there are also identification tools for ESTs and ORFs. A special feature of TrichDB is the ability to separately query repeat genes, which constitute a large fraction of the T. vaginalis genome.

TrichDB couples a comprehensive set of querying tools with extensively hyperlinked and highly-configurable displays of results. Numerous evidence types and data associated with a gene, including gene context, predicted protein, EST and BLAST alignments, and %GC content, can be displayed in a single graphic. Users can choose just which elements to show in a display, allowing them to generate purpose-built images for presentation.

Finally, TrichDB is also a repository for T. vaginalis genome statistics, data and methods source links, links to other Trichmonas community web sites, and daily updates of Trichomonas publications and genes deposited in Pubmed/Entrez. New users can learn to use the TrichDB/ApiDB sites via a set of online tutorials linked from the TrichDB front page.

We hope you will take time to explore TrichDB. Please use the link found at the bottom of every TrichDB page to send us feedback (Contact Us!). We look forward to hearing from you!
