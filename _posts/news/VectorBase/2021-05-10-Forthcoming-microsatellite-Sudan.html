Microsatellite data from Sudan corresponding to a forthcoming publication from Bashir Salim (Project PI) and Mohammed-Ahmed B. Elnour (Co-Investigator), both from the University of Khartoum (Sudan), and Jeffrey R. Powell (Co-Investigator) and Andrea Gloria-Soria (Co-Investigator), both from Yale University (USA). This data set will made publicly available in a forthcoming VectorBase release.

The research authors used 12 microsatellites loci to examine the population genetic and population history of Aedes aegypti in Sudan. Their findings indicate the presence of two subspecies, population structure and recent independent colonization events of Ae. aegypti in Sudan. Aedes aegypti mosquitoes were collected from eastern Sudan (Kassala and Port Sudan cities) and western Sudan (Al Fashir and Nyala cities), during November 2018- February 2020.

It has been pre-assigned MapVEu (Population Biology) project ID VBP0000762.
