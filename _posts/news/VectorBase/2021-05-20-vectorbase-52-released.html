We are pleased to announce the release of {{project}} 52.

The URL https://{{project}}.org directs you to the newest version of our interface. The site contains previous VectorBase.org data plus some new tools and functions in an updated and streamlined interface that empasizes easy access to help information. Navigate to https://legacy.{{project}}.org for the previous version of the interface. The legacy interface will remain active for at least one more release.

Release 52 Webinar

Join us on May 27, 2021 at 10am EDT for our Release 52 Highlights webinar where we will demonstrate and discuss new data and features in VEuPathDB 52. Register here

Content migration from legacy to beta VectorBase

We continue to migrate all the content from the legacy site before it is shut down, next on our list are cell line genomes (available in legacy BLAST), transcriptomes (available in the Expression Browser, Genome Browser track hubs, Downloads), proteome datasets and the Image Gallery. Please let us know which tool, resource, or dataset we might be missing or needs to be prioritized for migration.

New features in {{project}} 52

  • BLASTbeta tool – a new BLAST tool with multi-BLAST capabilities. Input a single sequence or several FASTA-formatted sequences at once. Our tool uses NCBI BLAST to find genes, genomic sequences, ESTs, or PopSet Sequences that have BLAST similarity to your input sequence. BLASTbeta video introduction (4 min)
  • Usage and performance metrics for VEuPathDB sites are now updated monthly.
  • Community annotations made in {{project}} via Apollo are now represented on gene pages in the Gene Models graphic. In addition, links from the gene page to Apollo are offered to facilitate community annotations. Example image. Please see our Apollo webinar for instruction.

New data in {{project}} 52

Transcriptomics

  • In VectorBase we have a total of 327 RNA-seq datasets. For the previous release, 113 RNAseq datasets were available in the search strategy system. For the current release we now have a total of 124 RNAseq datsets; more datasets will be included in following releases.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct errors in old data when necessary. Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
