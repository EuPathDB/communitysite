General Information

Giardia lamblia is a significant, environmentally transmitted, human pathogen and an amitochondriate protist. It is a major contributor to the enormous worldwide burden of human diarrheal diseases, yet the basic biology of this parasite is not well understood. No virulence factor has been identified. The Giardia lamblia genome contains only 12 million base pairs distributed onto five chromosomes. Its analysis promises to provide insights about the origins of nuclear genome organization, the metabolic pathways used by parasitic protists, and the cellular biology of host interaction and avoidance of host immune systems.

Since the divergence of Giardia lamblia lies close to the transition between eukaryotes and prokaryotes in universal ribosomal RNA phylogenies, it is a valuable, if not unique, model for gaining basic insights into genetic innovations that led to formation of eukaryotic cells. In evolutionary terms, the divergence of this organism is at least twice as ancient as the common ancestor for yeast and man. A detailed study of its genome will provide insights into an early evolutionary stage of eukaryotic chromosome organization as well as other aspects of the prokaryotic / eukaryotic divergence.

Additional Information

Genes were predicted using the computer programs CRITICA (1) and GLIMMER (2, 3) using a training set of published giardial genes from GenBank. The two programs collectively predicted 9,663 protein-coding genes after removal of predictions lacking proper start and stop codons. Where alternate start codons were possible for gene calls, the longest prediction was retained, including overlapping predictions on alternate reading frames. Each gene prediction was evaluated using independent statistical measures: TestCode (4), GeneScan (5) and the Codon Adaptation Index (6). Threshold scores were TestCode value of >4, GeneScan score of >9.7, or an in-frame codon usage score greater than the two out-of- frame scores (7).

In release 1.0, predictions were classified as genes if they had (1) similarity to known proteins at e-10 or better (2,370 predictions), (2) weak or no similarity, but transcriptional or proteomic evidence (2,753 predictions), or (3) no similarity or expression evidence, but high scores on two or more metrics (1,347 predictions), whether or not they exhibited similarity to known proteins or motifs. The very small number of recognized introns (4, to date) means that the start and stop of the gene model are identical to the corresponding ORF.

In release 1.1, we evaluated the degree and direction of overlap between genes and demoted many (1,611) to "deprecated genes". Most of the deprecated genes are short and have no known function.
