Citing Data Providers

Much of the data in PiroplamaDB is provided by independent researchers. For a list of the data integrated into PiroplasmaDB and the related publications see Data Sets. Please cite the authors/providers when you use their data.
Advisors

EuPathDB databases rely heavily on community input. Each project in EuPathDB benefits from the advice and mentoring of members from each specific community. We hold monthly organism calls for each of our databases with members of the scientific advisory teams and others from the community. Anyone interested in joining one of these conference calls is encouraged to contact us.
Funding

PiroplasmaDB is a member of pathogen-databases that are housed under the NIAID-funded EuPathDB Bioinformatics Resource Center (BRC) umbrella. The EuPathDB team is mainly distributed between the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania and the University of Georgia in Athens, Georgia.
Data Access Policy

The data at this site is provided freely for public use, through the contributions of many researchers including those involved in generating the genome sequences. The data is not guaranteed to be accurate as it often reflects the results of ongoing research. When using data obtained from PiroplasmaDB, it is important to cite the original publications and contributors of that data and PiroplasmaDB. Please see Citing PiroplasmaDB below.
Website Privacy Policy

We do not use or share any of your personal information for any purpose unrelated to the functionality of the websites; however, we do collect some information to help us understand how our sites are being used and to improve community support. See the Website Privacy Policy for details.
Citing PiroplasmaDB

When citing PiroplasmaDB itself, please use:

EuPathDB: a portal to eukaryotic pathogen databases. Nucleic Acids Res. 2010 Jan;38(Database issue):D415-9. Epub 2009 Nov 13.

When citing EuPathDB itself, please use:
EuPathDB: the eukaryotic pathogen genomics database resource. 2016 Nov 29. pii: gkw1105. [Epub ahead of print] Aurrecoechea C, et al.
