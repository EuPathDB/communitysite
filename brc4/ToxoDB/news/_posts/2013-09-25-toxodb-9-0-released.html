--- layout: post title: ToxoDB 9.0 Released category: [ToxoDB] --- We are pleased to announce the release of ToxoDB 9.0


New data in this release:

  • The following unannotated genomes of were integrated into the database. Additional information about all genomes may be viewed in the Dataset section of ToxoDB.  Note that many of these genomes have been graciously provided prepublication to the scientific community - please consult with the Principal Investigator before undertaking large scale analyses of the annotation or underlying sequence:

    • Hammondia hammondi H.H.34 (GenBank version 4-5-2012)
    • Toxoplasma gondii ARI (GenBank version 3-1-2012)
    • Toxoplasma gondii CAST (GenBank version 3-27-2012)
    • Toxoplasma gondii COUG (GenBank version 2-7-2013)
    • Toxoplasma gondii CtCo5 (GenBank version 7-17-2012)
    • Toxoplasma gondii GAB2-2007-GAL-DOM2 (GenBank version 8-8-2012)
    • Toxoplasma gondii TgCATBr5 (GenBank version 8-26-2011)
    • Toxoplasma gondii TgCATPRC2 (GenBank version 3-27-2012)
    • Sarcocystis neurona SN3 (Kissinger Lab 7-2-2013)

  • RNA sequencing of Eimeria tenella Houghton life cycle stages (unsporulated oocyst, sporulated oocyst, sporozoite and 2nd generation merozoite stages) graciously provided prepublication (Reid et al.).  

    • Go to the ToxoDB RNA Seq Evidence search page to search for genes that are differentially expressed between the life cycle stages. View sequencing read coverage using the GBrowse track - E.t. mRNAseq Transcriptome of Life Cycle Stages Coverage (Reid).

      • This example search strategy consists of 4 steps: In the first step Eimeria genes upregulated in merozoites compared to the other stages were identified.  In the second step a percentile search identifies genes unlikely to be expressed in unsporulated oocyst, sporulated oocyst and sporozoite stages. In the third step a percentile search identifies genes likely to be highly expressed in merozoites. The fourth step identifies the ortholog of these genes in Toxoplasma. 

http://toxodb.org/toxo/im.do?s=b76b14af75930e6c

    • Graphical representation of this data is available in the expression section of gene pages.  

    • Data from this experiment is available as coverage plots in the genome browser and evidence of intron spanning reads is indicated in the "Splice Site Junctions" track.  In this example a genome browser region shows two genes one that is differentially expressed in merozoites and the other with evidence of alternative gene models that differs from the official annotation.

  • RNA sequencing of Toxoplasma gondii ME49 during acute or chronic infection in mouse brain. This data was graciously provided  prepublication (Pittman, Aliota and Knoll). 

    • Search this data using the ToxoDB RNA Seq Evidence search page to compare the gene expression for parasites in 10 and 28 day old infections in mice.  This example search strategy identifies genes that are upregulated by at least 30-fold in chronically infected mice:

http://toxodb.org/toxo/im.do?s=b675ff1a72873ed4


    • View sequencing read coverage in the genome browser by turning on the track called "T.g. mRNAseq Transcriptome during acute or chronic infection in mouse brain Coverage (Knoll)".  This example genome browser region shows the gene encoding enolase 1 and 2.    

    • View the data from this experiment represented graphically in the expression section of individual genes on gene pages.

  • RNA sequencing of 25 Toxoplasma gondii strains during mouse macrophage infection (Minot et al.). 
 
    • Search for genes that are differentially expressed between strains using the RNA Seq Evidence search page. 

    • View sequencing read coverage in the genome browser by turning on the track called "T.g. mRNAseq Transcriptomes of 25 strains during murine macrophage infection Coverage". 

    • View percentile and expression data graphically in the expression section on individual gene pages.

  • Toxoplasma gondii quantitative proteomics data from Type II M4 oocyst and from VEG parasites during an infection of human macrophages – This data was graciously provided prepublication by Jonathan Wastling. 

    • Genes may be identified based on their differential expression using the Quantitative Mass Spec. Evidence search page.  

    • Expression levels are also represented graphically in the protein section of gene pages (Example: GRA7). 

New features:

GBrowse tracks remain open between sessions:


  • EuPathDB now stores GBrowse track configurations between sessions for registered users who log into any EuPathDB site. The EuPathDB login is now integrated with the GBrowse login so that your previous session’s GBrowse tracks are displayed each time you return to a EuPathDB site.

New streamlined look for choosing expression data sets (microarray, RNA-Seq):


  • A new interactive table for choosing expression data sets lists datasets, searches for each dataset and the organism that the data is aligned to. Use the table’s filter function to limit the data set list based on author name, parasite life stages, etc. Click on an icon under "Choose a search" to collapse the table and display the data set’s search page with its parameter list below the collapsed table.

On-page help for expression search pages:


  • Search parameters appear on the left and are arranged in a sentence that helps you choose parameter values.

  • A help graphic and text appear on the right. To help you interpret your parameters choices, the graphic changes to reflect your parameter settings.

Improved Strategy Panel for viewing and editing steps:


  • The graphic interface of the strategy panel has been updated.  View the results of a strategy step by clicking anywhere inside the box representing that step. To edit the parameter values for that step, click on the EDIT icon that appears in the corner of each strategy step box when hovering over the strategy panel.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary.  Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits. When search parameters change with a new release, we invalidate (Ø) the search and ask you to rerun it. When IDs are updated or removed, we map the old IDs to the new ones, remove the old IDs from your Basket, and leave your Favorites page alone.
