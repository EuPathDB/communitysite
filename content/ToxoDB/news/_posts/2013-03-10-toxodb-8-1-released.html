--- layout: post title: ToxoDB 8.1 Released category: [ToxoDB] --- We are pleased to announce the release of ToxoDB 8.1


New data in this release:

Proteomics of the lysine-acetylome of intra- and extracellular T. gondii (RH) tachyzoites (Jeffers et al. and Xue et al.).

  • Genes with mass spec evidence from intracellular and/or extracellular samples may be interrogated in ToxoDB.  For example, this two-step search strategy identifies genes that have mass spec evidence from intracellular parasites but no evidence from extracellular parasites.
  • Visit the Protein section of individual gene record pages to view proteomics data in tabulated and graphic form.
  • View peptides in a genomic context using the Genome Browser.

High throughput sequencing (HTS) of over 60  T. gondii strains (unpublished).  Paired end Illumina sequencing was performed on more than 60 strains spanning the 16 haplogroups identified in the T. gondii sequencing white paper. HTS SNP data may be searched in two ways:


    1. 1. Genes with specified SNP characteristics may be identified using the "HTS SNP Characteristics" query located under the "Population Biology" search category.  All SNPs are mapped onto the ME49 reference genome sequence.  Information regarding strain origin and haplogroups is available in this table.  

        2. Here is an example search strategy that identifies all genes that contain at least 10 nonsynonymous SNPs between TgMAS (congenital toxoplasmosis, Europe) and TgRAY (congenital toxoplasmosis, USA) that are supported by at least 50 sequencing reads.

    2. 2. SNPs may be identified using any of the queries located under the heading "SNPs" in the "Identify other data types" section.  SNPs may be identified based on their IDs, Gene IDs,  Genomic location and strain. 

New Functionality:

PCR Primer Design tool added to GBrowse.

Design PCR primers to any region of the genome using this interactive, graphic interface. To reach this tool, open the ToxoDB Genome Browser at your genomic region of interest, choose "Design PCR primers" from the dropdown menu and click "GO".  To design primers, focus on a PCR product size range by highlighting the area on the scale, then enter your preferred PCR parameters in the text boxes and click Design Primer.

Note: Our bimonthly database releases incorporate new data and correct old data when necessary.  Changes in annotation and new experimental data may slightly alter your search results by increasing or decreasing the number of hits.
