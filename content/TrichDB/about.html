General Information

The Trichomonas vaginalis genome project was undertaken at The Institute for Genomic Research (TIGR) directed by Jane Carlton in collaboration with Patricia Johnson and the Trichomonas research community. Funding for the project was provided initially by a UO1 grant from NIAID/NIH and subsequently with funds from NIAID's Microbial Sequencing Centers. The Burroughs Wellcome Fund and The Ellison Medical Foundation also provided funds for several community meetings. The project took 3 years 6 months to complete and cost an estimated $3.7 million. The paper describing the T. vaginalis genome was published as the front cover article in Science in January 2007.

The T. vaginalis sequencing effort was complemented by EST data from the Chang Gung T. vaginalis Systems Biology Project, directed by Petrus Tang and supported by grants from Chang Gung Memorial Hospital and Taiwan Biotech Company Limited. Details of the T. vaginalis ESTs generated by Chang Gung University can be obtained from the website http://TvXpress.cgu.edu.tw.

For a list of the data integrated into TrichDB and the related publications please see Data Sets.

Please check our annual EuPathDB Workshop at UGA exercises to see the scope of data and functionality in our sites.
Citing Data Providers

Much of the data in TrichDB is provided by independent researchers. For a list of the data integrated into TrichDB and the related publications see Data Sets. Please cite the authors/providers when you use their data.

Funding

TrichDB is a member of pathogen-databases that are housed under the NIAID-funded EuPathDB Bioinformatics Resource Center (BRC) umbrella. The EuPathDB team is mainly distributed between the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania and the University of Georgia in Athens, Georgia.
Data Access Policy

The data at this site is provided freely for public use, through the contributions of many researchers including those involved in generating the genome sequences. The data is not guaranteed to be accurate as it often reflects the results of ongoing research. When using data obtained from TrichDB, it is important to cite the original publications and contributors of that data and TrichDB. Please see Citing TrichDB below.
Website Privacy Policy

We do not use or share any of your personal information for any purpose unrelated to the functionality of the websites; however, we do collect some information to help us understand how our sites are being used and to improve community support. See the Website Privacy Policy for details.
